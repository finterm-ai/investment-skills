# Valuation and Price Discipline

Use these cards as evidence-bounded analytical prompts derived from Buffett and
Berkshire writings. Apply them to supplied evidence, preserve each card’s limits, and
return unresolved findings when prerequisites are missing.

Read this file when selecting a valuation method, normalizing the base, testing growth,
sensitivity, or price.
Load only the cards selected by the router; do not load the whole module by default.
Abbreviated corroboration citations resolve in the [source key](00-source-key.md).

## Complete Cards

<a id="v01"></a>

### V01 — Define Intrinsic Value as a Range of Owner Cash

- **Decision question:** What conservative range represents the present value of cash
  owners can remove over the business’s remaining life?
- **Guidance:** Model owner cash, reinvestment, timing, and discounting explicitly and
  express value as a range reflecting uncertainty.
- **Use when:** Evidence supports an explicit owner-cash valuation rather than only
  asset or relative checks.
- **Analytical actions:** Define removable owner cash; build supported cash-flow and
  terminal scenarios; state discounting assumptions and range-invalidating variables.
- **Output:** An intrinsic-value range tied to operating, reinvestment, timing, and
  discount assumptions.
- **Limits:** Analysts can produce different ranges; no universal discount rate or
  terminal method applies, and unbounded cash flows leave value unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1994 annual letter** — *defines*: intrinsic value discounted
    owner cash.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1999 annual letter** — *implements*: estimate intrinsic value
    as a range.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.D; EWB §V.E; OM1996; BH2014.

<a id="v02"></a>

### V02 — Match the Valuation Method to Business Economics and Use Shortcuts Only as Checks

- **Decision question:** Which method fits predictability, assets, growth, and capital
  structure, and what can simpler measures legitimately check?
- **Guidance:** Select the method from the economics and evidence; treat multiples and
  book value as checks unless linked to owner cash or realizable assets.
- **Use when:** Before valuing an asset-heavy, cyclical, financial, high-growth, or
  structurally changing business.
- **Analytical actions:** Classify value drivers and forecastability; justify the
  primary method and checks; explain why rejected methods misstate economics.
- **Output:** A valuation-method memo naming the primary method, checks, rejected
  alternatives, and unresolved method risk.
- **Limits:** No method repairs unforecastable inputs; if no method has defensible
  inputs, valuation remains unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1989 annual letter** — *defines*: intrinsic value discounted
    future cash flows.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1989 annual letter** — *qualifies*: book value is an imperfect
    proxy for intrinsic value.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.D; OM1996; BH2002; BH2014.

<a id="v03"></a>

### V03 — Select a Defensible Normalized Base for Valuation

- **Decision question:** Which reconciled earnings or owner-cash base should valuation
  capitalize across the relevant cycle?
- **Guidance:** Derive the base from F05 while preserving segment differences, cycles,
  unusual items, acquisition effects, and genuine costs.
- **Use when:** Valuation depends on normalized earnings, margins, cash conversion, or
  cycle position.
- **Analytical actions:** Compare reported and normalized periods; justify cycle and
  segment weights; show alternative bases and valuation consequences.
- **Output:** A valuation-base bridge with selected range, rejected periods, cycle
  rationale, and sensitivities.
- **Limits:** Cycle endpoints are uncertain and structural change can invalidate
  history; an indefensible period leaves the base unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1983 annual letter** — *implements*: separate operating
    earnings from unusual gains.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2019 annual letter** — *qualifies*: separate operating earnings
    from investment gyrations.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.B; BH2002; BH2023.

<a id="v04"></a>

### V04 — Value Growth Only Through Incremental Capital Economics

- **Decision question:** Does projected growth increase future owner cash after funding
  its capital requirements?
- **Guidance:** Credit growth only through supported changes in owner cash and
  incremental returns; reduce expectations for capital hunger or return decay.
- **Use when:** Growth contributes materially to valuation or thesis.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconcile growth with incremental capital and owner cash;
  compare returns with opportunity cost; sensitize scale, runway, and funding.
- **Output:** A growth-value bridge separating revenue growth, required capital,
  incremental return, and owner-cash contribution.
- **Limits:** Future returns are unobserved; unbounded funding or incremental returns
  leave growth value unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1992 annual letter** — *supports*: growth creates value only
    above cost of incremental capital.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2000 annual letter** — *supports*: growth affects value only
    through cash flow economics.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.D; EWB §III.C; CM50-2015; LOW ch.10 (cable-TV rejection
  case).

<a id="v05"></a>

### V05 — Expose Assumptions, Sensitivities, and Missing Evidence

- **Decision question:** Which assumptions drive the range, how fragile are they, and
  which missing facts block a conclusion?
- **Guidance:** Make operating, reinvestment, discounting, and terminal assumptions
  explicit; test plausible combinations and identify invalidating evidence.
- **Use when:** Every material valuation, especially when forecast or model dependence
  is high.
- **Analytical actions:** Rank drivers by impact and uncertainty; run supported scenario
  combinations; map each missing input to the blocked conclusion.
- **Output:** A sensitivity and missing-evidence register tied to the value range and
  invalidation conditions.
- **Limits:** Sensitivity cannot validate bad assumptions or assign unsupported
  probabilities; unsupported bounds require no value conclusion.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1999 annual letter** — *implements*: estimate intrinsic value
    as a range.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2020 annual letter** — *illustrates*: Precision Castparts
    overpayment from earnings overestimate.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.D; BH2002; BH2014.

<a id="v06"></a>

### V06 — Require a Margin of Safety Proportionate to Uncertainty

- **Decision question:** Is the price-value gap adequate for uncertainty, resilience,
  financing, and downside shown by the evidence?
- **Guidance:** Compare price with the conservative end of a supported range and require
  more room for greater uncertainty; a discount cannot cure fragility.
- **Use when:** A defensible value range and downside analysis exist and price is in
  scope.
- **Analytical actions:** Quantify scenario gaps; tie required room to named
  uncertainty; test whether the discount survives adverse assumptions.
- **Output:** A price-value table with scenario gaps, uncertainty drivers, and an
  adequate, inadequate, or unresolved analytical finding.
- **Limits:** No universal discount applies; missing price or value leaves the result
  unresolved, and the finding is not trade authority.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Warren Buffett’s 1984 essay “The Superinvestors of Graham-and-Doddsville”** —
    *defines*: margin of safety.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2020 annual letter** — *illustrates*: Precision Castparts
    overpayment from earnings overestimate.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.D; BH2025; LOW ch.5 (franchise-value margin evolution); LOW
  ch.8 (Washington Post discount case).

# Management, Governance, and Culture

Use these cards as evidence-bounded analytical prompts derived from Buffett and
Berkshire writings. Apply them to supplied evidence, preserve each card’s limits, and
return unresolved findings when prerequisites are missing.

Read this file when testing conduct, operating contribution, incentives, controls,
governance, or succession.

## Complete Cards

<a id="m01"></a>

### M01 — Evaluate Integrity and Candor Without Letting Likability Substitute for Evidence

- **Decision question:** Does management demonstrate integrity and candor when
  disclosure or restraint is personally costly?
- **Guidance:** Treat character separately from performance and give more weight to
  timely bad news, honest postmortems, fair treatment, and costly restraint than
  reputation or charisma.
- **Use when:** Trustworthiness, disclosure quality, related-party conduct, or a thesis
  dependent on managerial character.
- **Analytical actions:** Compare favorable and adverse-period communications; identify
  costly choices; separate conduct from operating and allocation results.
- **Output:** A conduct ledger with supported, adverse, and unresolved observations by
  period.
- **Limits:** Character is partly unobservable and vulnerable to halo and hindsight;
  candor never replaces controls.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2024 annual letter** — *supports*: managerial candor is a
    governance signal.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1989 annual letter** — *qualifies*: admirable managers are
    necessary but not sufficient.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.F; BPL1969; OM1996; BH2002.

<a id="m02"></a>

### M02 — Judge Operating Competence in Economic Context

- **Decision question:** How much performance reflects execution rather than inherited
  economics, industry conditions, leverage, or tailwinds?
- **Guidance:** Assess execution against the opportunity set and constraints management
  faced; do not infer competence solely from outcomes.
- **Use when:** Comparing managers across periods, peers, cycles, or businesses with
  materially different economics.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconcile operating drivers with external conditions; compare
  controllable outcomes; separate execution from allocation and character.
- **Output:** A contextual performance bridge identifying controllable contribution,
  external effects, and unresolved attribution.
- **Limits:** Counterfactual performance is unobservable and peer sets may be poor;
  leave attribution unresolved when causes cannot be separated.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2007 annual letter** — *supports*: select managers for brains
    passion and integrity.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1989 annual letter** — *qualifies*: admirable managers are
    necessary but not sufficient.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §I.E; CM50-2015; BH2014.

<a id="m03"></a>

### M03 — Judge Capital-Allocation Skill Separately from Operations

- **Decision question:** Has management converted retained and redeployed resources into
  superior long-term per-share owner value?
- **Guidance:** Evaluate allocation separately from operations and compare material uses
  with contemporaneous alternatives using per-share economics.
- **Use when:** Retention, acquisitions, securities, debt changes, repurchases,
  distributions, or inaction materially affects the thesis.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconstruct decisions and alternatives; compare expected and
  realized per-share economics; conduct postmortems without hindsight substitution.
- **Output:** A dated allocation scorecard with amount, channel, rationale,
  alternatives, outcome, and unresolved counterfactual.
- **Limits:** Outcomes do not fully reveal decision quality and one successful decision
  does not establish future skill.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2021 annual letter** — *implements*: compare capital allocation
    routes by return and scale.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1994 annual letter** — *supports*: allocate capital by per
    share intrinsic value not near term EPS.
- **Corroboration:** CM50-2015; OM1996; BH2014.

<a id="m04"></a>

### M04 — Inspect Incentives, Ownership Alignment, and Owner Treatment

- **Decision question:** What behavior do pay, ownership, shared downside, spending, and
  reporting arrangements actually reward?
- **Guidance:** Map incentives to controllable risk-adjusted value and test
  owner-alignment claims through conduct, exposure, and fair treatment.
- **Use when:** Compensation, control, related parties, dilution, discretionary
  spending, or minority-owner treatment is material.
- **Analytical actions:** Decompose pay and ownership; test charges for capital and
  risk; compare stated alignment with dilution, spending, disclosure, and treatment.
- **Output:** An incentive-and-alignment map linking mechanisms to rewarded behavior,
  owner consequences, and conflicts.
- **Limits:** Concentrated ownership can create control risk and no pay formula fits
  every role; opaque exposure leaves alignment unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1994 annual letter** — *implements*: tie manager pay to
    controllable operating results.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2003 annual letter** — *qualifies*: material owner alignment
    supports director independence but does not guarantee it.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §I.E; OM1996; FL2025.

<a id="m05"></a>

### M05 — Test Culture and the Institutional Imperative Through Behavior

- **Decision question:** Which behaviors does the organization repeatedly attract,
  reward, normalize, and reproduce?
- **Guidance:** Treat culture as a system expressed through hiring, incentives,
  forecasts, costs, imitation, and reactions to mistakes—not declared values.
- **Use when:** A thesis depends on durable organizational behavior, acquisition
  appetite, cost discipline, forecasting, or decentralization.
- **Analytical actions:** Identify repeated behaviors; trace reinforcing mechanisms;
  search for actions contradicting the declared culture.
- **Output:** A culture-mechanism map with reinforcing behaviors, contradictions, and
  change indicators.
- **Limits:** Culture is neither automatic nor irreversible; leave durability unresolved
  when evidence is anecdotal or leader dependent.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1989 annual letter** — *defines*: institutional imperative.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2000 annual letter** — *supports*: long term growth forecasts
    can corrupt managerial behavior.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.F; BH2002; BH2014.

<a id="m06"></a>

### M06 — Assess Decentralization, Controls, and Bad-News Flow

- **Decision question:** Does authority sit with capable operators while audit,
  compliance, escalation, and capital control remain effective?
- **Guidance:** Evaluate autonomy and control together; decentralization requires
  capable managers, protected escalation, and explicit retained controls.
- **Use when:** Decentralized groups, holding companies, autonomous subsidiaries, or
  multilayer information flow.
- **Analytical actions:** Map delegated and retained authorities; test audit,
  compliance, whistleblower, and bad-news channels; review detection and correction of
  errors.
- **Output:** An authority-and-control matrix with escalation paths, failure examples,
  and unresolved dependencies.
- **Limits:** Centralization can also fail and visible mistakes may be an accepted cost;
  absent incident evidence leaves control quality unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1991 annual letter** — *implements*: decentralize operations
    centralize capital allocation.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2004 annual letter** — *qualifies*: decentralized companies
    need protected whistleblower channel.
- **Corroboration:** CM50-2015; OM1996; LOW ch.15 (two-level control structure).

<a id="m07"></a>

### M07 — Test Substantive Board Independence and Power

- **Decision question:** Can the board understand, challenge, and act against management
  when owner interests require it?
- **Guidance:** Judge independence by competence, economic independence, information,
  deliberation, and willingness to oppose or replace management—not formal labels.
- **Use when:** Board oversight, CEO power, deals, compensation, conflicts, or
  controlling ownership affects the thesis.
- **Analytical actions:** Map skills, ties, information, and voting power; inspect
  dissent mechanisms; review behavior during conflicts or underperformance.
- **Output:** A board-power assessment separating formal status from demonstrated
  capability and action.
- **Limits:** Ownership and private sessions do not create judgment; unobservable
  conflict behavior leaves independence unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2003 annual letter** — *supports*: business savvy is
    indispensable for corporate directors.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2019 annual letter** — *implements*: boards should hear
    informed opposition to acquisitions.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §I.B; BH2014; FL2025.

<a id="m08"></a>

### M08 — Assess Role-Specific Succession and Control Continuity

- **Decision question:** Can leadership, operating, investment, and control roles
  transfer without losing capability or fair treatment?
- **Guidance:** Assess succession by role, horizon, emergency readiness, delegated
  experience, and control-transfer mechanics; a name is not proof.
- **Use when:** Key-person dependence, controlling ownership, aging leadership, estate
  plans, voting arrangements, or role concentration.
- **Analytical actions:** Separate role successions; inspect emergency and long-term
  plans and staged responsibility; map voting, estate, and sale mechanics.
- **Output:** A role-specific continuity map with successor evidence, control
  transitions, triggers, and unresolved dependencies.
- **Limits:** Private plans may remain undisclosed and legal or tax mechanics can
  change; names or assurances alone leave readiness unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2005 annual letter** — *defines*: succession role separation.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2010 annual letter** — *implements*: document emergency
    successors with strengths and weaknesses.
- **Corroboration:** CM50-2015; BH2014; FL2025.

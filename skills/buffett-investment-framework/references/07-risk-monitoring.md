Use these cards as evidence-bounded analytical prompts derived from Buffett and
Berkshire writings. Apply them to supplied evidence, preserve each card’s limits, and
return unresolved findings when prerequisites are missing.

conditions, or invalidation.

## Complete Cards

<a id="r01"></a>

### R01 — Define Risk by Permanent Owner Harm or Forced Action

- **Decision question:** What could permanently reduce owner purchasing power, future
  consumption, or the ability to wait?
- **Guidance:** Define risk by permanent owner outcomes and forced-action paths;
  volatility matters when it interacts with liquidity, solvency, mandate, or horizon.
- **Use when:** Risk analysis dominated by beta, volatility, or generic labels.
- **Analytical actions:** Define owner, horizon, and harm; trace permanent-loss and
  forced-action mechanisms; separate quotation volatility from impairment.
- **Output:** An owner-risk taxonomy with harm, mechanism, horizon, trigger, and
  unresolved probability.
- **Limits:** Volatility matters for constraints and short horizons; this definition
  proves neither concentration nor equity safety.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2011 annual letter** — *defines*: investment risk as purchasing
    power loss probability.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1993 annual letter** — *implements*: assess real risk through
    purchasing power and business factors.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.C; OM1996; BH2022.

<a id="r02"></a>

### R02 — Stress Leverage and Liquidity Under Correlated Adversity

- **Decision question:** Can obligations and operating capacity survive several
  plausible stresses arriving together?
- **Guidance:** Stress debt, liquidity, refinancing, collateral, and operating needs
  jointly; do not rely on averages or uninterrupted credit access.
- **Use when:** Material leverage, maturity concentration, cyclical cash flow, liquidity
  dependence, or forced-action exposure.
- **Analytical actions:** Establish unrestricted liquidity and reserve; combine
  operating, market, collateral, and refinancing stresses; identify first failure.
- **Output:** A correlated-stress table with sources, uses, reserve breach, refinancing
  dependence, and unresolved inputs.
- **Limits:** No universal threshold applies and ring-fenced cash flows may support
  different debt only when legal separation is verified.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2018 annual letter** — *implements*: preserve calamity
    liquidity before acquisitions.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2010 annual letter** — *supports*: leverage can destroy
    survival when refinancing disappears.
- **Corroboration:** BPL1967; OM1996; BH2014; BH2023.

<a id="r03"></a>

### R03 — Stress Contingent Obligations, Derivatives, Models, and Counterparties

- **Decision question:** Could nonlinear claims, model errors, collateral demands, or
  weakest-link counterparties exceed liabilities or available liquidity?
- **Guidance:** Reconstruct the obligation and counterparty chain, challenge model
  population and incentives, and stress amount, timing, correlation, collateral,
  refinancing, and settlement together.
- **Use when:** Derivatives, pensions, reserves, guarantees, structured claims, or
  model-dependent liabilities.
- **Analytical actions:** Build the obligation and collateral chain; challenge
  structural breaks; stress timing, correlation, liquidity, and counterparties.
- **Output:** A contingent-obligation ledger and tail map with ranges, dependencies, and
  unresolved exposures.
- **Limits:** Product expertise is often necessary; do not estimate absent exposure
  merely to complete the analysis.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2003 annual letter** — *supports*: derivatives company
    disclosures cannot reveal the full risk.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2008 annual letter** — *implements*: test history based models
    for changes in the underlying universe.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.G; BH2002.

<a id="r04"></a>

### R04 — Test Inflation Exposure Through Pricing Power and Capital Needs

- **Decision question:** Does nominal growth become real owner value after pricing lags,
  replacement capital, working capital, tax, and financing?
- **Guidance:** Trace inflation through revenue, costs, price, volume, required capital,
  financing, and owner cash rather than labeling a hedge.
- **Use when:** Long-duration forecasts, inflation-sensitive inputs, fixed-price claims,
  capital intensity, or an asserted inflation hedge.
- **Analytical actions:** Map price-cost-volume timing; estimate replacement and working
  capital; compare nominal with real owner cash.
- **Output:** An inflation-transmission map and real-owner-cash sensitivity with
  unresolved variables.
- **Limits:** Pricing can reduce volume and productive assets can require heavy capital;
  asset labels do not resolve the result.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2011 annual letter** — *defines*: investment risk as purchasing
    power loss probability.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2011 annual letter** — *qualifies*: buy currency securities
    only for unusual mispricing or rate opportunities.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.C; BH1981.

<a id="r05"></a>

### R05 — Preserve Compounding by Minimizing Avoidable Friction

- **Decision question:** How much underlying return is lost to fees, taxes, turnover,
  advice, and unnecessary activity?
- **Guidance:** Measure avoidable friction before crediting active intervention while
  keeping survival and reinvestment quality as prior conditions.
- **Use when:** Strategy comparison, manager evaluation, turnover, taxes, or claims that
  gross performance equals owner return.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconcile gross to net; compare with a lower-friction
  alternative; compound measured drag over the horizon.
- **Output:** A friction bridge showing costs and cumulative owner impact.
- **Limits:** Low cost does not make a weak strategy sound and access, mandate, tax, and
  benchmarks differ.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1983 annual letter** — *supports*: market turnover imposes
    owner friction costs.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2013 annual letter** — *supports*: frequent trading and advice
    costs reduce aggregate investor returns.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §III.A; EWB §III.D; BH2014.

<a id="r06"></a>

### R06 — Test Whether an Exit Condition Has Been Met

- **Decision question:** Have economics, thesis evidence, integrity, or opportunity cost
  changed enough to satisfy a predeclared exit condition?
- **Guidance:** Compare current evidence with the original thesis and conditions;
  distinguish deterioration and superior alternatives from price reaction.
- **Use when:** Hold, sell, or thesis-update review after material evidence or a
  proposed exit.
- **Analytical actions:** Compare original and current assumptions; test economics,
  integrity, value, tax, and alternatives; classify each condition.
- **Output:** An exit-condition assessment with evidence and unresolved questions, but
  no trade instruction.
- **Limits:** Long holding and sale for higher-priority uses are in tension; no
  condition automatically authorizes a trade.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Warren Buffett’s 2015 50th-anniversary essay** — *qualifies*: consider inaction
    before selling a prosperous business.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2009 annual letter** — *illustrates*: security sales funded
    higher priority cash deployment.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §IV.E; BPL1967; BH2014 (Tesco exit-delay case).

<a id="r07"></a>

### R07 — Monitor Assumptions, Change Indicators, and Invalidation Conditions

- **Decision question:** Which observations would strengthen, weaken, revise, or retire
  each material assumption?
- **Guidance:** Maintain a compact assumption register with evidence-linked indicators
  and invalidation conditions; update from decision-relevant evidence rather than
  undirected news or quotes.
- **Use when:** Ongoing monitoring, periodic thesis review, or material new operating,
  governance, financing, or industry evidence.
- **Analytical actions:** Register assumptions, indicators, and cadence; classify new
  evidence; mark assumptions strengthened, weakened, revised, retired, or unresolved.
- **Output:** A thesis-monitoring register with dated evidence, status changes, and
  blocked conclusions.
- **Limits:** Indicators can be noisy or lagging and no update automatically produces a
  buy, sell, or hold action.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2013 annual letter** — *implements*: ignore daily prices and
    monitor productive output.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1996 annual letter** — *qualifies*: do not sell fine businesses
    on scary news alone.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §II.E; BPL1967; BH2014.

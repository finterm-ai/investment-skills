# Capital Allocation

Use these cards as evidence-bounded analytical prompts derived from Buffett and
Berkshire writings. Apply them to supplied evidence, preserve each card’s limits, and
return unresolved findings when prerequisites are missing.

Read this file when reviewing retention, distributions, repurchases, issuance,
acquisitions, or reserves.
Load only the cards selected by the router; do not load the whole module by default.

## Complete Cards

<a id="c01"></a>

### C01 — Optimize Long-Term Per-Share Owner Value

- **Decision question:** How does the decision change each continuing owner’s long-term
  economics after financing, dilution, tax, risk, and alternatives?
- **Guidance:** Use per-share intrinsic-value effects as the objective; do not
  substitute aggregate growth, EPS, book value, or corporate size.
- **Use when:** Material deployment, financing, payout, acquisition, issuance, or
  discretionary spending.
- **Analytical actions:** Bridge enterprise to per-share effects; trace dilution,
  claims, taxes, downside, and opportunity cost; separate accounting optics from
  economics.
- **Output:** A per-share value bridge showing value contributed and surrendered,
  affected owners, assumptions, and unresolved effects.
- **Limits:** Value is estimated and stakeholder or legal constraints may dominate; an
  unsupported bridge stays unresolved and authorizes nothing.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1994 annual letter** — *implements*: allocate capital by per
    share intrinsic value not near term EPS.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1982 annual letter** — *qualifies*: EPS dilution is not value
    dilution.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §I.A; OM1996; BH2022; LOW ch.10 (Washington Post per-share
  case).

<a id="c02"></a>

### C02 — Set the Allocation Reserve Before Optional Return

- **Decision question:** What capital and true liquidity must remain unavailable so
  operations and obligations survive correlated adversity?
- **Guidance:** Establish operating, claims, covenant, regulatory, and calamity reserves
  before optional uses; accept foregone return when refinancing dependence would make
  survival fragile.
- **Use when:** Optional deployment competes with working capital, debt, collateral,
  claims, regulated capital, or credible stress needs.
- **Analytical actions:** Inventory obligations, timing, restrictions, and liquid
  assets; stress simultaneous demands; state protected reserve and supported surplus.
- **Output:** A reserve schedule with required liquidity, stress uses, funding,
  restrictions, and unresolved surplus.
- **Limits:** No universal ratio applies and nominal liquidity may disappear;
  unverifiable access makes the amount unavailable, not surplus.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2018 annual letter** — *implements*: preserve calamity
    liquidity before acquisitions.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2003 annual letter** — *qualifies*: stable ring fenced utility
    cash flows can support subsidiary debt.
- **Corroboration:** OM1996; BH2014; FL2025.

<a id="c03"></a>

### C03 — Compare the Full Capital-Allocation Menu and Available Channels

- **Decision question:** Which route offers the strongest supported owner outcome after
  tax, risk, scale, timing, financing, and friction?
- **Guidance:** Compare internal investment, acquisitions, securities, debt change,
  repurchase, payout, and inaction on one explicit owner-value basis.
- **Use when:** More than one discretionary route is available or management omits
  alternatives.
- **Analytical actions:** Enumerate feasible routes; normalize owner cash, downside,
  duration, tax, scale, control, and reversibility; rank only comparable evidence.
- **Output:** A capital-route matrix with assumptions, constraints, ranges, downside,
  scale, and unresolved alternatives.
- **Limits:** No universal hurdle or probability method applies; survival precedes
  ranking and the matrix authorizes no deployment.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2021 annual letter** — *implements*: compare capital allocation
    routes by return and scale.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1987 annual letter** — *supports*: select by after tax
    mathematical expectation.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §IV.D; OM1996; BH2014.

<a id="c04"></a>

### C04 — Retain and Reinvest Earnings Only for Productive Deployment

- **Decision question:** Is retained capital likely to increase per-share owner value
  more than distribution or another feasible route?
- **Guidance:** Treat retained earnings as owner capital that must earn its keep through
  prospective incremental earning power and later accountability.
- **Use when:** Material retention for organic growth, maintenance presented as growth,
  or central redeployment.
- **Analytical actions:** Separate maintenance from growth; estimate incremental returns
  and capacity; compare later outcomes with the original case and alternatives.
- **Output:** A retention ledger linking amounts to uses, expected economics, realized
  outcomes, and unresolved attribution.
- **Limits:** Returns can be delayed and hard to attribute; if maintenance and growth
  cannot be separated, the test is unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1978 annual letter** — *implements*: condition retention on
    profitable reinvestment.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2016 annual letter** — *supports*: retained earnings must
    increase earning power.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §III.C; OM1996; BH2014; LOW ch.15 (subsidiary retention test).

<a id="c05"></a>

### C05 — Return Surplus Capital When No Better Use Exists

- **Decision question:** After protected reserves and productive uses, is capital
  genuinely surplus?
- **Guidance:** Treat payout as the consequence of no better supported deployment, not a
  fixed preference; distinguish distribution from central redeployment.
- **Use when:** Mature or capital-light operations generate cash beyond maintenance,
  growth, obligations, and resilience needs.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconcile distributable cash after C02; compare C03
  alternatives; show tax, control, and owner-treatment effects of payout channels.
- **Output:** A surplus-capital ledger with amounts blocked, deployable, distributable,
  or unresolved.
- **Limits:** Legal, tax, contractual, and balance-sheet limits may block payout; an
  incomplete reserve or opportunity set means no surplus finding.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1984 annual letter** — *implements*: Berkshire dividend policy
    uses value creation threshold.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2007 annual letter** — *qualifies*: redeploy cash from high
    return low reinvestment businesses.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §III.C; BH2014; LOW ch.15 (distribution threshold case).

<a id="c06"></a>

### C06 — Repurchase Only Below Value with Surplus Capacity and Material Effect

- **Decision question:** Will repurchase materially improve continuing-owner value
  without weakening resilience or unfairly transferring value?
- **Guidance:** Require conservative undervaluation, protected liquidity, fair
  disclosure, superior alternatives, and meaningful per-share effect.
- **Use when:** An issuer proposes, conducts, or defends a material repurchase.
- **Analytical actions:** Compare price with value; recheck capacity and alternatives;
  quantify shares retired, financing, per-share effect, and transfers.
- **Output:** A repurchase worksheet with value, capacity, materiality, fairness,
  alternatives, and unresolved assumptions.
- **Limits:** Value is uncertain and above-value purchases can transfer wealth; the
  worksheet authorizes no repurchase or trade.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1980 annual letter** — *implements*: repurchase below intrinsic
    value.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1999 annual letter** — *qualifies*: above value repurchases
    transfer wealth from continuing owners.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §IV.B; BH2014; BH2022; LOW ch.10 (Washington Post repurchases).

<a id="c07"></a>

### C07 — Issue Shares Only Value for Value

- **Decision question:** Does issuing equity give owners at least as much durable value
  and resilience as the interest surrendered?
- **Guidance:** Analyze issuance as a partial sale of owner assets and compare value
  surrendered with assets, claims extinguished, or resilience received.
- **Use when:** Stock-financed acquisitions, compensation issuance, conversion,
  recapitalization, or balance-sheet repair.
- **Analytical actions:** Estimate value and ownership surrendered; value consideration
  and resilience received; reconcile to continuing-owner value rather than optics.
- **Output:** An issuance exchange bridge with ownership transfer, proceeds, financing
  effects, and per-share result.
- **Limits:** Issuance can rationally improve resilience and both sides are uncertain;
  missing estimates leave the exchange unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1982 annual letter** — *implements*: share issuance value for
    value rule.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1992 annual letter** — *illustrates*: stock financed bank deal
    destroyed value.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §IV.A; OM1996; LOW ch.12 (stock-deal dilution cases).

<a id="c08"></a>

### C08 — Evaluate Acquisitions by Quality, Price, Financing, Alternatives, and Postmortems

- **Decision question:** Does the complete exchange improve continuing-owner value under
  defensible stand-alone and downside cases?
- **Guidance:** Evaluate target economics, management, price, financing, dilution,
  alternatives, and realized outcomes together, not EPS or size.
- **Use when:** A proposed or completed acquisition, merger, or major asset purchase.
- **Analytical actions:** Underwrite stand-alone quality and owner cash; build a value
  exchange and financing bridge; preserve assumptions for post-closing comparison.
- **Output:** An acquisition sheet with stand-alone value, consideration, financing,
  alternatives, downside, per-share effect, and postmortem fields.
- **Limits:** Good businesses can be overpaid for and synergies may matter; unbounded
  economics or consideration leaves the assessment unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1985 annual letter** — *implements*: Berkshire whole business
    acquisition criteria.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2017 annual letter** — *implements*: evaluate acquisitions on
    all equity basis.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §IV.D; OM1996; BH2014.

<a id="c09"></a>

### C09 — Counter Acquisition-Process Bias

- **Decision question:** Has commitment manufactured confidence through selective
  information, incentives, or optimistic assumptions?
- **Guidance:** Require an informed opposing case challenging seller information,
  forecasts, turnarounds, synergies, adviser incentives, and deal hunger.
- **Use when:** A material transaction with committed leadership, seller-controlled
  information, or synergy and turnaround dependence.
- **Analytical actions:** Build the stand-alone downside before synergies; map
  incentives and information; run a premortem and preserve assumptions.
- **Output:** An independent deal-challenge memo listing contested assumptions,
  adverse-selection risks, opposing evidence, and unresolved conditions.
- **Limits:** Seller advantage does not imply fraud, deals do not universally fail, and
  broad acquisition priors cannot determine a specific case.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1995 annual letter** — *supports*: most acquisitions damage
    buyers and seller projections mislead.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2017 annual letter** — *supports*: deal hunger recruits biased
    forecasts and synergies.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §IV.A; EWB §IV.D; BH2014; LOW ch.12 (acquisition ego cases).

## Complete Cards

<a id="f01"></a>

### F01 — Reconstruct Economic Substance from Accounting

- **Decision question:** What ownership, cash-flow, and obligation economics lie behind
  the reported result?
- **Guidance:** Begin with audited figures and reconcile every adjustment back to them;
  accounting rules are inputs, not permission to discard costs.
- **Use when:** Accounting form, rule changes, acquisition accounting, noncash charges,
  consolidation, or management metrics may obscure economics.
- **Analytical actions:** Inventory policies and changes; reconcile totals to the
  analytical view; explain the economic reason for every adjustment.
- **Output:** A reported-to-economic bridge with source, amount, rationale, and
  unresolved items for each adjustment.
- **Limits:** Adjustments can introduce bias and false precision; unsupported
  substitutions or an unreconciled bridge remain unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1982 annual letter** — *supports*: accounting numbers begin not
    end valuation.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2001 annual letter** — *illustrates*: goodwill rule change
    raises reported not economic earnings.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.B; EWB §V.D; OM1996; BH2014.

<a id="f02"></a>

### F02 — Estimate Owner Earnings After All Real Costs

- **Decision question:** How much cash can owners remove without weakening competitive
  position or unit volume?
- **Guidance:** Restore noncash charges only when they are not economic depletion and
  subtract maintenance capital, required working capital, compensation, and recurring
  costs; use supported ranges.
- **Use when:** Cash removability or distributable earning power matters to valuation,
  reinvestment, or payout analysis.
- **Analytical actions:** Show every adjustment; estimate maintenance capital and
  working capital across a representative period; sensitize judgmental components.
- **Output:** A reconciled owner-earnings range with assumptions, sensitivities, and
  blocked components.
- **Limits:** Maintenance and growth overlap and depreciation or amortization may
  misstate depletion; unbounded material needs leave the result unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1986 annual letter** — *defines*: owner earnings.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2000 annual letter** — *supports*: depreciation represents real
    maintenance capital cost.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.D; BH2002; BH2014; BH2023.

<a id="f03"></a>

### F03 — Separate Maintenance, Growth, Restricted, and Distributable Capital

- **Decision question:** Which capital preserves economics, funds growth, remains
  trapped, or is actually distributable?
- **Guidance:** Classify capital by economic function before treating cash flow as owner
  value; required, catch-up, restricted, or low-return reinvestment is not freely
  distributable.
- **Use when:** Capital-intensive, regulated, cyclical, rapidly growing, or structurally
  restricted businesses.
- **Analytical actions:** Classify spending and working capital; reconcile restrictions
  and reserves; test whether claimed growth spending only preserves economics.
- **Output:** A schedule of maintenance, growth, catch-up, restricted, and distributable
  amounts or ranges.
- **Limits:** Categories overlap and management labels may be unreliable; unbounded
  maintenance or restrictions leave distributability unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1986 annual letter** — *defines*: owner earnings.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2007 annual letter** — *qualifies*: rapid growth low return
    capital hunger is worst business model.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.C; OM1996; BH2023.

<a id="f04"></a>

### F04 — Look Through Partial Ownership, Segments, and Holding Structures

- **Decision question:** What economics accrue through minority interests, segments, and
  holding structures despite reported presentation?
- **Guidance:** Attribute relevant results to distinct businesses and interests,
  reconcile to reported totals, and credit retained investee earnings only with
  dilution, tax, and productive-deployment evidence.
- **Use when:** Minority holdings, conglomerates, equity-method interests, or materially
  different segments.
- **Analytical actions:** Map ownership and segments; calculate look-through economics,
  taxes, and dilution; reconcile to consolidated reporting.
- **Output:** A look-through and segment schedule with ownership, reported treatment,
  economic attribution, and differences.
- **Limits:** Retention can destroy value and segment definitions can obscure economics;
  inadequate data leaves attribution unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1997 annual letter** — *defines*: look through earnings.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1985 annual letter** — *supports*: segment data essential for
    multiline business analysis.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.B; EWB §V.F; OM1996; BH2014.

<a id="f05"></a>

### F05 — Build a Reconciled Normalized Earning-Power Base

- **Decision question:** What recurring earning power belongs to each distinct business
  across a representative period?
- **Guidance:** Bridge unusual, market-driven, cyclical, and acquisition effects without
  erasing genuine costs; preserve reconciliation and period.
- **Use when:** Before valuation or comparison when results contain cycles, unusual
  items, marks, acquisitions, or heterogeneous segments.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconcile by segment and period; classify adjustments; produce
  base and adverse normalized ranges.
- **Output:** A normalized earning-power bridge by segment, period, and adjustment with
  reconciled totals.
- **Limits:** Normalization can cherry-pick and structural change can make history
  unrepresentative; an unbounded cycle leaves the base unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1983 annual letter** — *implements*: separate operating
    earnings from unusual gains.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2003 annual letter** — *implements*: analyze diversified
    enterprises by economic segment and through time.
- **Corroboration:** BH2014; BH2022.

<a id="f06"></a>

### F06 — Measure Returns on Tangible and Incremental Capital

- **Decision question:** What normalized unleveraged returns does the business earn on
  required tangible capital, and what has additional capital produced?
- **Guidance:** Measure historical tangible and incremental returns independently of
  leverage and acquisition premiums; keep measurement separate from prospective
  durability and value.
- **Use when:** Business quality, reinvestment, growth value, or capital intensity is
  material.
- **Analytical actions:** Reconcile operating profit and tangible capital; calculate
  incremental returns over defensible intervals; separate organic investment,
  acquisitions, leverage, and goodwill.
- **Output:** A historical return schedule with denominators, intervals, adjustments,
  and sensitivities.
- **Limits:** Denominators and lags are judgmental and historical returns may not
  persist; an indefensible bridge remains unresolved.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1992 annual letter** — *supports*: best and worst businesses by
    incremental capital returns.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2013 annual letter** — *supports*: returns on unleveraged net
    tangible assets reveal business economics.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.C; OM1996; BH2014; LOW ch.20 (USAir capital-intensity case).

<a id="f07"></a>

### F07 — Reconcile Cash Conversion and Hidden or Long-Duration Obligations

- **Decision question:** What disclosed or implied claims could absorb cash materially
  beyond reported earnings or liabilities?
- **Guidance:** Reconcile cash conversion and inventory debt, reserves, pensions,
  derivatives, collateral, guarantees, and uncertain promises by amount, timing, range,
  and liquidity demand.
- **Use when:** Accruals, long-tail claims, pensions, derivatives, guarantees,
  collateral, or refinancing obligations are material.
- **Analytical actions:** Build a cash bridge; inventory obligations and timing;
  reconcile reported amounts to ranges and assumptions.
- **Output:** An obligation ledger with amount or range, timing, model dependence, cash
  trigger, and unresolved exposure.
- **Limits:** Product mechanics matter and terminal cash can differ from interim marks;
  do not invent absent ranges.
- **Source basis:**
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 1992 annual letter** — *supports*: cash accounting can conceal
    growing future liabilities.
  - **Berkshire Hathaway 2003 annual letter** — *qualifies*: derivatives company
    disclosures cannot reveal the full risk.
- **Corroboration:** EWB §V.H; BH2002.
